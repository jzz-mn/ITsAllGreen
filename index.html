    Sustainable Development Goals

    The Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) are a set of 17 interconnected goals established by the United Nations in 2015 to guide global efforts towards sustainable development. They aim to end poverty, protect the planet, and ensure peace and prosperity for all by 2030, and require collaboration and partnership between governments, civil society, the private sector, and individuals to drive positive change and create a more sustainable future.
